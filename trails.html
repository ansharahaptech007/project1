Trails and Treks to Help You Navigate With Ease

Find the Trails here!

Pakistan is home to some of the most stunning national parks and natural landscapes in the world, making it a haven for trail enthusiasts and trekking aficionados. The country's national parks, including the famous ones like Deosai National Park, Khunjerab National Park, and the Hingol National Park, offer a variety of trekking trails that traverse diverse terrains, from lush green meadows and alpine lakes to rocky ridgelines and towering mountain peaks. Deosai, often referred to as the "Land of Giants," is known for its vast plateaus and pristine beauty, offering trekking routes that provide panoramic views of the surrounding snow-capped mountains and valleys. The rugged terrains of the Khunjerab National Park, located in the Karakoram Range, feature trails that pass through high-altitude landscapes, where trekkers may spot wildlife like snow leopards and ibex. Hingol National Park, situated along the Makran Coastal Highway, provides a unique trekking experience, with trails that wind through deserts, riverbeds, and limestone cliffs, leading trekkers to the famous Hingol River and the impressive mud volcanoes. The lush forests and steep slopes of the Shandur National Park are also a sought-after destination for trekkers, offering challenging trails that lead to the highest polo ground in the world.

Central Karakoram National Park

Click on Image to get Complete Guide

Central Karakoram National Park, located in the heart of the Karakoram Range, is one of the most awe-inspiring trekking destinations in Pakistan. The park is a vast expanse of rugged mountains, glaciers, and alpine meadows, offering a range of challenging and scenic trails for trekkers seeking an extraordinary adventure. The most famous trek in the region is the trek to K2 Base Camp, which takes adventurers deep into the Karakoram, offering close-up views of some of the highest peaks in the world, including K2 itself. The trek is renowned for its challenging terrain, including moraine fields, glaciers, and icy river crossings, but the rewards are unmatched: trekkers are treated to panoramic views of towering peaks, vast glaciers, and the serene beauty of the Baltoro Glacier. Another prominent trek in the area is the Gondogoro La Pass, which connects the Baltoro Glacier to the Hushe Valley, offering trekkers a challenging ascent to one of the highest points in the park, followed by a descent that reveals spectacular vistas. The park is also home to a diverse range of wildlife, including the elusive snow leopard, ibex, and markhor, adding an element of excitement and mystery to any trek. For those looking for a true test of endurance and a deep connection with one of the most remote and pristine wildernesses on Earth, the trails and treks of Central Karakoram National Park provide an unparalleled experience.

Along the way, trekkers are often treated to glimpses of traditional Chitrali culture, as many routes pass through small villages where locals maintain their centuries-old way of life.
