Location And Occupied Area

Ayubia National Park is located in the Abbottabad District of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan. It is situated in the northeastern part of the country, within the beautiful hills and valleys of the Galyat range, which lies between the cities of Abbottabad and Murree. The park spans an area of approximately 3,312 hectares (about 8,200 acres) and is part of the larger Ayubia National Park Conservation Area, which includes the famous Pipeline track. The park is known for its diverse ecosystems, ranging from lush green forests to rugged mountain terrain, and is home to a variety of flora and fauna. The area is famous for its rich biodiversity, including species such as the Himalayan Black Bear, Leopard, Musk Deer, and various species of birds, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.

Flora And Vegetation

Ayubia National Park is known for its rich and diverse flora and vegetation, owing to its varied topography and climate. The park is predominantly covered by temperate forests, including coniferous trees like Pine, Cedar, and Fir, which thrive at higher altitudes. At lower elevations, the park features broadleaf forests with trees such as Oak, Maple, and Bamboo. The vegetation is lush and dense, with various shrubs, herbs, and wildflowers, especially during the monsoon season. These include species like Rhododendrons, Azaleas, and a variety of grasses that contribute to the park’s vibrant ecosystem. The diverse plant life supports a wide range of wildlife, from herbivores like Musk Deer to various species of birds. The rich flora of Ayubia National Park not only adds to the park’s aesthetic beauty but also plays a crucial role in maintaining the ecological balance of the region.

Fauna And Species Found

Ayubia National Park, located in the Abbottabad District of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan, is a stunning area known for its rich biodiversity and breathtaking landscapes. Established in 1984, the park spans over 33,000 hectares and is part of the larger Galyat Range. It is renowned for its lush forests, which are home to a variety of wildlife species. Among the notable animals found in Ayubia National Park are the HIMALAYAN BLACK BEAR, MARKHOR, LEOPARD, and MUSK DEER. The park also hosts populations of JACKAL, WILD BOAR, and HIMALAYAN IBEX in the higher altitudes. Ayubia is also a birdwatcher's paradise, with species such as the HIMALAYAN VULTURE, EUROPEAN HONEY BUZZARD, and PEREGRINE FALCON soaring across the skies. The diverse flora of the park includes a mix of coniferous trees, like pine, spruce, and deodar, along with oak and walnut trees in lower elevations. The park is also famous for the AYUBIA CHAIRLIFT and the PIPELINE TRACK, which offer visitors a chance to explore the region's beauty. Ayubia National Park is a critical conservation area, protecting both wildlife and their habitats while also promoting sustainable tourism.
