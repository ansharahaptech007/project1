Location And Occupied Area

Central Karakoram National Park (CKNP) is located in the Gilgit-Baltistan region of northern Pakistan, encompassing the breathtaking landscapes of the Karakoram Range. It is one of the largest protected areas in Pakistan, covering an area of approximately 10,000 square kilometers (3,861 square miles). The park stretches across the districts of Skardu, Gilgit, and Hunza-Nagar, and is home to some of the world’s highest peaks, including K2 (8,611 meters), the second-highest mountain on Earth. CKNP's vast expanse also includes numerous glaciers, such as the Baltoro, Biafo, and Siachen, making it a crucial region for glaciology and water resources in the region. Its strategic location and unique geography make it a haven for biodiversity, eco-tourism, and mountaineering enthusiasts from around the globe.

Flora And Vegetation

Central Karakoram National Park (CKNP) boasts a diverse range of flora and vegetation, shaped by its unique geography and varying altitudes. The park's vegetation transitions from dry alpine shrubs to lush green meadows as the elevation changes, supporting a rich ecological spectrum. At lower altitudes, the valleys feature juniper, birch, and willow trees, which form essential habitats for local wildlife and provide soil stability. Moving higher, alpine meadows dominate, with a vibrant display of wildflowers such as blue poppies, asters, and primroses during the short growing season. In the uppermost regions, where conditions are harsher, vegetation becomes sparse, with mosses, lichens, and hardy cushion plants clinging to rocky terrains. These plants play a vital role in preventing soil erosion and contributing to the nutrient cycle in the fragile high-altitude ecosystems. CKNP’s vegetation not only enhances its scenic beauty but also sustains a delicate ecological balance, supporting various herbivores and acting as a foundation for the survival of its rare and endangered fauna.

Fauna And species found

Central Karakoram National Park (CKNP) is a biodiversity hotspot, home to a wide variety of fauna, including some of the world’s most iconic and endangered species. Among the mammals, the snow leopard, a symbol of high-altitude wilderness, is the park's flagship species. Other large mammals include the Himalayan ibex, blue sheep, and the elusive brown bear, all of which thrive in the park's rugged, remote landscapes. Smaller mammals, such as marmots and hares, are also common and play critical roles in the park’s food chain. The park is equally renowned for its avian diversity, making it a haven for birdwatchers. Birds of prey such as the golden eagle, Himalayan griffon vulture, and lammergeier soar high above the park, while ground-dwelling species like the chukar partridge and snowcock inhabit the rocky terrain. The region also supports rare reptiles and amphibians adapted to the extreme climate, further enhancing its ecological significance.
