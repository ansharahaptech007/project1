MARKHOR: The Crowns Of North

Pakistan's national animal, is a strikingly majestic wild goat known for its spiraled horns and rugged beauty. Found in the steep, rocky terrain of the Himalayan, Karakoram, and Hindu Kush mountain ranges, it thrives in regions like Chitral Gol National Park, Kirthar National Park, and other protected areas across northern and western Pakistan. Markhors are highly agile, skillfully navigating the rugged landscapes and feeding on grasses, leaves, and shrubs. Their distinctively twisted horns can grow up to 160 centimeters in males, making them a symbol of resilience and strength. These animals play a vital ecological role by helping maintain vegetation balance in their habitats. Unfortunately, markhors have faced significant threats due to overhunting, poaching, and habitat loss, leading to their classification as an endangered species. However, conservation programs and community-based initiatives, such as those implemented by the Wildlife Department of Pakistan and international organizations like WWF, have contributed to a remarkable recovery in their population. Today, the markhor stands as a symbol of successful wildlife conservation, inspiring efforts to protect the country's rich biodiversity.

Big cats Of Deosai/ Ayubia National Park

Snow leopards, known as the "ghosts of the mountains," are an elusive and majestic species found in the rugged, mountainous regions of Pakistan. These big cats inhabit several national parks, including the Khunjerab National Park, Deosai National Park, and Chitral Gol National Park, which serve as crucial sanctuaries for their conservation. Snow leopards are perfectly adapted to survive in harsh alpine environments, with thick, smoky-grey fur patterned with rosettes, long bushy tails for balance and warmth, and powerful limbs to navigate steep and rocky terrain. In Pakistan, these cats are primarily found in the Karakoram, Hindu Kush, and Himalayan ranges. The presence of snow leopards in national parks underscores the importance of these protected areas in maintaining biodiversity and supporting the ecosystem of the region. Conservation efforts, such as those spearheaded by the Snow Leopard Foundation and the World Wildlife Fund (WWF), have been instrumental in protecting this endangered species through initiatives like community-based conservation programs, reducing human-wildlife conflict, and curbing poaching.
